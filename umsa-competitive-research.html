Key Workday uMSA Differentiators

What sets Workday apart vs. oCIO vendor agreements (based on Universal MSA and Universal Amendment)

🛡️ Breach Remediation Carveout (Clause 8.3)

Only Workday contractually commits to pay reasonable and documented costs for forensic investigation, breach notifications, credit monitoring (1 year), and call center (1 year) when unauthorized disclosure is caused by Workday's breach of its security, privacy, or data protection obligations. These obligations are excluded from the liability cap (Cl. 8.1(D)). No other vendor in this set does this.

⏱️ 48-Hour Breach Notification (Clause 5.3)

Workday commits to notify within 48 hours or any shorter period required by Law upon awareness of a Security Breach, and to conduct a root cause analysis and share results and remediation plan upon request. Snowflake has 72-hour DPA commitment but took 45 days in 2024; GCP and OpenAI use undefined "without undue delay."

📄 Single Universal MSA for All Products

One agreement (Order Form + MSA + Security Exhibit + DPE + Product Terms) covers all Workday products; Order Form takes precedence (Cl. 10.14). Competitors use layered, multi-document stacks with URL-incorporated documents that can change mid-contract.

🔐 Customer Content Only to Provide the Service; Customer Owns Outputs

MSA Cl. 3(a): Workday has the right to use Customer Content only to provide the Service. Amendment: Customer owns all Outputs from AI Systems; Workday does not use Customer Content to train external AI/ML models — a clear, contract-level commitment vs. tier-dependent or policy-based "no training" elsewhere.

🔍 Security Exhibit and Audit Reports

Security program conforms to the Security Exhibit and Audit Reports; Workday makes Audit Reports available via self-service or upon written request. No update may materially decrease protections during the Term (Cl. 5.1; Amendment §5). Replaces per-customer audit negotiation.

📋 Predictable Product Terms (30-Day Notice)

Product Terms may not materially decrease security/privacy commitments; changes do not become effective until 30 days after publication (MSA definition; Amendment §11). Contrast with vendors that incorporate terms by URL with no minimum notice before material changes.

📤 Retrieval and Transition

Up to 60 days at no cost to retrieve Customer Content in machine-readable format (Cl. 9.2). Optional up to 3 months Transition Period after termination if requested before termination (Cl. 9.3).
